01 Elegant Welcome Card Recommended

The safest institutional pick. A single vertical card with VERA logo, "Secure access · Verified" pill, warm display-serif headline, and three clean info rows. It reuses the exact chromatic system the Jury Information page already has (blue / amber / green meta chips), so it feels like a true precursor to the form — not a separate experience.

MotionStaggered fade-up on load (card, then meta rows, then CTA — ~700ms total). Nothing ambient, nothing moving after that.
02 Immersive Glass Experience

The most atmospheric option. A frosted glass card floats over a slow-drifting aurora with a handful of light dust particles, and an animated conic ring on the "Access granted" pill mirrors the QR scan moment. Still light-themed, still restrained, but delivers an unmistakable "arrival" feeling — the first second feels like an event rather than a page.

MotionAurora drifts on a 14s loop, dust particles rise and fade on 11s, CTA has a 4.5s sheen pass. Card enters with a soft blur-clear.
03 Enterprise Dashboard Prelude

Split-panel layout — narrative and bullet checklist on the left, a structured session summary panel with "Jury access verified" shield on the right. This is the "serious SaaS" read: reads like a pre-flight summary, shows everything upfront, and signals to institutional clients (TEDÜ and future adopters) that the system is infrastructure-grade. Heavier visually, but unmatched for trust.

MotionCard fades/rises once; green "SESSION · ACTIVE" dot pulses; shield checkmark draws on reveal. No ambient motion.
04 Signature Brand Moment

A minimal, editorial-grade brand moment — large VERA mark inside a soft-glow halo, an italic display headline ("Evaluate the future of Engineering"), the group count rendered as a typographic hero. This is the variation that will show best in screenshots and marketing surfaces; functionally thinner than 01 and 03, but the most memorable first impression by a wide margin.

MotionAura pulses on 6s, halo sweeps on 5s conic rotation, content enters with a 1.1s rise. The mark is the hero — everything else is still.
Recommended path → Ship Concept 01 as the default arrival screen — it continues the visual grammar of the existing Jury Information page and carries no risk of feeling disconnected. Layer in Concept 04's hero moment as an A/B variant for marketing-facing previews (demo environment, landing-page embeds). Concept 02's aurora + glass is a premium choice worth revisiting once the product has a signature "loading" motion vocabulary. Concept 03 is the right answer when VERA lands its first true enterprise client and the session-summary read is load-bearing.
